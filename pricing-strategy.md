# Pricing Strategy
## Recommended Pricing Models with Justifications

---

## 1. Fixed-Price Productized Services (PRIMARY)

**What:** Pre-defined scope, timeline, and price. Client pays for the outcome, not the hours.

**Why this wins:**
- Clients hate hourly billing uncertainty. Fixed price removes friction.
- You get paid for value, not time. A 5-day project that saves them 20 hours/week is worth more than your hourly rate.
- Easier to sell. "₹50,000 for a 3-day sprint" is concrete. "₹5,000/hour for 10 hours" is vague.
- Forces you to scope well. You learn fast what takes time and what doesn't.
- Builds trust. No invoice surprises.
- Scales better. You can templatize and reuse.

**Current fixed-price packages:**
- AI Internal Assistant Quick Start: ₹75,000 (5 days)
- AI Internal Assistant Professional: ₹1,50,000 (10 days)
- AI Feature Prioritization Sprint: ₹50,000 (3 days)
- AI Voice Agent Pilot: ₹2,00,000 (10 days)
- AI Workflow Automation: ₹50,000–1,50,000 (3–7 days)

**When to use:** Always for defined-scope projects. This is your default.

---

## 2. Retainer Pricing (SECONDARY)

**What:** Monthly fixed fee for ongoing work, support, or maintenance.

**Why this wins:**
- Predictable recurring revenue. The holy grail of freelance.
- Builds long-term relationships. Clients stay longer.
- Lower sales effort. One close, 12 months of revenue.
- Better cash flow. You know what you're making next month.
- Perfect for: maintenance, content updates, monitoring, monthly sprints.

**Recommended retainer packages:**
- **AI Assistant Maintenance:** ₹15,000/month (monitoring, bug fixes, minor updates)
- **Monthly AI Sprint:** ₹25,000/month (1 mini-sprint per month, 2-3 features)
- **Fractional AI PM:** ₹50,000/month (10-15 hours/week, strategy + execution)

**When to use:** After first project. Offer during handoff. "Want me to keep this running for ₹15K/month?"

---

## 3. Discovery Workshop Pricing (TERTIARY)

**What:** One-time paid audit or workshop before the main project.

**Why this wins:**
- Low-risk entry point for clients. ₹5,000–10,000 is easier to say yes to than ₹75,000.
- Qualifies leads. Clients who pay for discovery are serious.
- Creates urgency. Workshop findings become the proposal for the main project.
- You get paid to write proposals. No more free spec work.
- Positions you as consultant, not vendor.

**Recommended discovery offerings:**
- **AI Readiness Audit:** ₹5,000 (1-hour call + 2-page report on where AI fits in their ops)
- **Process Automation Audit:** ₹10,000 (2-hour workshop + automation roadmap)
- **Feature Prioritization Mini-Sprint:** ₹15,000 (1-day analysis of top 5 features)

**When to use:** For cold leads, complex projects, or when the client isn't sure what they need.

---

## 4. Hourly Pricing (AVOID)

**Why not:**
- Punishes efficiency. The faster you work, the less you earn.
- Creates adversarial dynamic. Client watches the clock. You watch the clock.
- Hard to scale. More hours = more burnout, not more revenue.
- No incentive to build reusable systems.
- Clients optimize for cost reduction, not value creation.

**Exception:** Emergency fixes or ad-hoc consulting. Even then, quote a fixed price for the fix.

**If forced to quote hourly:** ₹3,000–5,000/hour for AI consulting. But always try to convert to fixed-price.

## Pricing Psychology

**Anchor high, negotiate down:**
- Always present the Professional tier first.
- If they push back, offer the Quick Start tier.
- Never negotiate below 70% of your stated price. If they want lower, reduce scope.

**Payment terms:**
- Fixed-price projects: 50% upfront, 50% on delivery.
- Discovery workshops: 100% upfront (small amount, low risk).
- Retainers: 100% monthly, paid in advance.
- First-time clients: 100% upfront for projects under ₹50,000.

**Money-back guarantee:**
- Offer on discovery workshops: "If you don't find this actionable, full refund."
- Offer on Feature Sprint: "If the analysis doesn't change your roadmap, full refund."
- Do NOT offer on implementation projects. Too much risk.

**Raising prices:**
- After 3 clients at a tier, raise by 20%.
- After 5 clients, raise by another 20%.
- After 10 clients, you have enough data to price based on value, not time.
